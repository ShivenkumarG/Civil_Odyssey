What is PMP:

the greatest depth of precipitation for a given duration meteorologically possible for a design watershed or a given storm area at a particular location at a particular time of year, with no allowance made for long-term climatic trends.

  • Enveloping Curve:
    It's a curve that encompasses all potential peak frequency factors, with the highest achievable frequency factor closely aligning with the curve's peak, which could also manifest in similar regions or basins.
  • Frequency Factor:
    In PMP calculation, the frequency factor indicates the maximum distance from the mean in terms of standard deviation units.

Advantages of Including Climate Change:

Overall, incorporating climate change data into calculations of probable maximum precipitation enables more robust and sustainable infrastructure development, contributing to the long-term well-being and safety of societies.

  • Long-term Planning:
    By factoring in climate change, planners can develop infrastructure that remains effective and functional over its intended lifespan, even as environmental conditions evolve.

Why is it Important?

  • It's crucial to estimate the maximum probable precipitation when designing dams and other infrastructure because it helps ensure that these structures can withstand the most extreme weather conditions they might encounter. By accurately predicting the maximum amount of rainfall likely to occur within a certain period, engineers can design infrastructure with appropriate safety margins to prevent failures or catastrophic damage during severe storms or flooding events.
